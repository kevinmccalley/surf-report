# BREAK POSTS — Kevin's Guest Blog

Kevin writes short, honest posts about specific breaks. These are not marketing. They are genuine local knowledge that happens to be exactly what surfers search for before a trip.

---

## What these posts are

A surfer planning a trip to Supertubos will Google "surfing Supertubos conditions" or "best time to surf Baleal". Right now, those searches return forum threads from 2015 and generic travel sites. Kevin has local proximity to these breaks combined with 3.5 years of Groundswell data. These posts own those searches and build trust with exactly the right audience.

---

## Format — same every time

**Title:** `Surfing [Break Name]: Conditions, Tides, and What the Data Says`

**Word count:** 600–900 words. Not less (Google won't rank it), not more (Kevin doesn't need to write an essay).

**Structure — always this order:**

### 1. The break (100–150 words)
What kind of wave is it. Who it's for. One honest sentence about what makes it special and one about what makes it tricky.

### 2. Best conditions (150–200 words)
Swell direction, size, period. Wind direction. Tide stage. What the forecast needs to show for it to be worth going. Kevin writes this from memory and experience — this is the most valuable part.

### 3. What the data says (150–200 words)
Pull up 3 years of historical data on Groundswell. What does October actually look like vs November? What's the seasonal pattern? One or two specific observations from the data. Link naturally to groundswell.surf — not a sales pitch, just "here's where I pulled this."

### 4. The honest take (100–150 words)
What the app gets right here. What it sometimes misses. Which direction tends to be overforecast. Any local knowledge the model can't capture. This is the section that builds the most trust.

### 5. One-line close
"[Break name] is in [Groundswell's covered spots / searchable at groundswell.surf]." That's it. No CTA, no "sign up now."

---

## Frequency

**1 post per month.** Consistent beats frequent. One well-written break post every month compounds into a library of genuine surf intelligence over time.

**Don't batch-publish.** One post, published, then wait a month. Google responds better to steady cadence than bursts.

---

## Consistency strategy

Always the same format. Always the same structure. Always the same title pattern.

This matters for two reasons:
1. Kevin never stares at a blank page — he knows exactly what to write
2. Google recognizes the pattern and ranks subsequent posts faster

After 12 months: 12 break posts, each ranking for a specific location search. That's a permanent SEO asset that grows without maintenance.

---
